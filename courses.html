COURSES

We intend to build a community from different disciplines (and professions) who draw as a part of their thinking process. Our courses are designed for those who are keen to draw, with various levels of experience.

Take a look at our courses below and click on them for more details and to book.

We want to enable an informal but focused atmosphere and so each studio based course has a maximum of 12 participants working with 2 tutors... more

Our courses will start in June, 2021. As we get Eye to Pencil up and running, we are offering a variety of courses both daytime and evenings. We welcome feedback and queries, so get in touch if you have any questions. We will be adjusting our courses for 2022 with this feedback in mind. Choose one of the courses below to get more information on the dates, course description, prices and how to apply.

We hope to run the courses in our drawing studio, as described, however if it is necessary due to COVID regulations, we will will offer our courses online. Here are our COVID precautions for the Drawing Studio.


A SPACE TO DRAW
On Mondays we are offering you a space to draw in the studio on an hourly basis. This is a chance to develop your own work in an unguided way, draw from a still life we will have set up or draw whatever you choose. Come and join us to draw together. Studio drawing materials are included.


TRANSCRIPTION
In each of these fortnightly sessions we will focus on one artwork, making quick and slow drawings transcribing some aspect of the artwork. This will follow by a short talk about the art historical context for the work, using our large screen and projector.


LUNCHTIME DRAWING IN THE PARK
We are hoping for sunny days for lunchtime drawing in July , in Spa Fields EC1, just around the corner from the Drawing Studio (we will post dates here and on instagram depending on the weather). We will bring pencils, drawing boards and paper. Come and join us to draw whatever you like.


DRY MATERIALS
Over the 5 sessions we will explore a wide range of dry drawing materials in combination with different types of paper. Through the process becoming more sensitive to the feel and potential of the materials at hand.


WET MATERIALS
Over 5 sessions we will explore a range of wet drawing materials including: ink; watercolour and mixed media. This course complements the Dry Materials course.


DRAWING BETWEEN THE MUSEUM & STUDIO
We will start each session in one of London’s public collections, with our sketchpads and pencils, then spend the afternoon back in the Drawing Studio where we will pick up on threads from the morning’s session, developing ideas further through drawing and discussion.


REVISITING BASICS
This course is designed to free up and extend your range of drawing. Through 4 days of drawing, looking and discussion, you will become more aware of the visual choises we make when drawing.
