The Studio

The studio will be a space to draw together and a platform for visual exchange. It will be a space for observing and looking, discussing drawing, collaborating, sharing ideas where all of us can learn something new through drawing. Our sessions are planned to guide visually intuitive research, led through specific drawing tasks.

Studio Manifesto

What it is

A studio Investigation into process Exchange Generating lively discussion Space for quiet concentration Non hierarchical Renewal/ revisiting (revision is key and people will not be satisfied with results - PART OF learning process - what can we learn through the activity) Visual Space Nonlinear but structured Angels/ Shapeshifters


Time for looking
Looking with the history of drawing interwoven into practise
Emotional qualities - giving courage and encouragement
Love drawing share in encouraging and none judgemental Analytical but non-judgmental
Sensitivity to materials and scale
Learning through making - learning to talk through making
Mistakes are part of the process
Being aware that you are choosing - Awareness of approach that is chosen
Discuss the work with mutual respect
A sense of value for drawing in itself

What is it not


Not a school
No nudes
Not academic
Not how to
Art history as a disconnected discipline
Not about making finished art works
Not outcome based
No binary good/bad
No prizes no medals no certificates

CODE OF CONDUCT/ Housekeeping

Students will need to sign this code of conduct when booking a class There must be a mutual respect and a use of constructive language for discussion and critique. We insist upon respect for diversity and appropriate sexual conduct. Unless otherwise instructed drawing time is quiet, mobile phones should be kept in silent mode and try to avoid interruptive use It is important to follow any health and safety instructions carefully.
