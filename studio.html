The Studio

The studio will be a space to draw together and a platform for visual exchange. It will be a space for observing and looking, discussing drawing, collaborating, sharing ideas where all of us can learn something new through drawing. Our sessions are planned to guide visually intuitive research, led through specific drawing tasks.

Our approach to drawing is to give everyone confidence in their ability to communicate through drawing or sketching. We believe in learning through making and that drawing is part of a visual thinking process which often entails revision. It often isnâ€™t easy to translate visual ideas into verbal explanations, however, discussing and sharing our observations and experiences is an important part of our studio activity. This is why we feel that looking and making should be interwoven with the history of drawing. Our method of teaching is an extension of a twenty year conversation between an artist and an art historian where the distinctions between these disciplines have become more blurry.

We want to create a studio space that can be both, a quiet and focused place to work and also a place for lively debate. Drawing together is the catalyst for our discussions and we genuinely want to learn what the common ground and the limitations of drawing seem to be in different facets of life.
