Courses

Central to all our courses is drawing together. We will guide and encourage you through specific drawing tasks which are visually intuitive.

Our inaugural courses start in June. As we get Eye to Pencil up and running we will trial a variety of courses and timings. We welcome feedback and queries, so get in touch if you have any questions. We will be adjusting our courses for 2022 with this feedback in mind. Choose one of the courses below to get more information on the dates, course description, prices and how to apply.

We want to enable an informal but focused atmosphere and so each course has a maximum of 12 participants working with 2 tutors.

Revisiting Basics

This course is designed to free up and extend your range of drawing. Through 4 days of drawing, looking and discussion, you will become more aware of the visual choises we make when drawing.
The online version of this course is similar in approach but has tasks specifically designed for online platforms. We would like to open up the course for less local participants.


Dry Materials

Over the 5 sessions we will explore a wide range of dry drawing materials in combination with different types of paper. Through the process becoming more sensitive to the feel and potential of the materials at hand.


Wet Materials

This course complements the Dry Materials course, but this time we will explore a range of wet drawing materials including: ink; watercolour and mixed media.


Drawing between the Museum and Studio

We will start each session in one of London’s public collections, with our sketchpads and pencils, then spend the afternoon back in the Drawing Studio where we will pick up on threads from the morning’s session, developing ideas further through drawing and discussion.


Transcription

These fortnightly sessions begin with Antje and Luci talking about one artwork.The conversation which we would like you to join in, starts with an art historian and a painter looking from similar but different perspectives. We will follow by making quick and slow drawings transcribing some aspect of the artwork.


Open Mondays

We are offering you a space to draw on an hourly basis. This is a chance to develop your own work in an unguided way, draw from a still life we will have set up or draw whatever you like. Come and join us to draw together.


Lunchtime Drawing in the Park

We are hoping for sunny days for lunchtime drawing in Spa Fields EC1, just around the corner from the Drawing Studio. We will bring pencils, drawing boards and paper. Come and join us to draw whatever you like.
